White House criticizes House Republicans for inaction on Ukraine aid

By Norah O'Donnell, John Dickerson, Margaret Brennan

Edited by Debra Young, Michael Henry, Carmen Pancoast

Updated on: July 8, 2024 / 3:38 PM EDT / CBS News

Washington D.C. — The White House has ramped up criticism of House Republicans and Speaker Mike Johnson in recent days over their failure to bring up a Senate-passed bill that would provide aid to Ukraine, where conditions have grown more dire, as well as Israel and Taiwan.

White House Communications Director Ben LaBolt said in a statement Tuesday morning that "Speaker Johnson and House Republicans must act" on the foreign aid, noting that "time is of the essence."

"House Republicans are on Day 5 of an early, undeserved vacation while their inaction does escalating damage to our national security," LaBolt said. "We must stop Putin from continuing to murder innocent women and children while threatening our own national security."
